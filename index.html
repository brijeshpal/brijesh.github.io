About

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Information Technology at the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Allahabad, advised by Prof. S. Venkatesan and Prof. Krishna Pratap Singh.

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and cybersecurity. I work on few-shot and meta-learning methods for detecting zero-day and evolving ransomware, and on using deep learning and large language models to reason about malicious program behaviour.

Previously, I received an M.Sc. in Computational Science and Applications from Banaras Hindu University, where I worked on climate modelling for temperature and rainfall prediction, and a B.Sc. (Hons.) in Computer Science from Ramanujan College, University of Delhi.

Research Interests

My research on trustworthy AI for cybersecurity sits at the intersection of few-shot learning, deep representation learning, and malware analysis, and asks questions like:

  • How can we detect previously unseen ransomware families when only a handful of samples are available1?
  • Can variational meta-learning and prototypical methods produce embeddings that generalise across malware behaviours1?
  • How do we build deep learning models that are fair and unbiased across sensitive attributes and deployment settings2?
  • Can large language models reason about API-call sequences, binaries, and program behaviour well enough to help defenders?
  • How should few-shot detectors adapt to zero-day threats and concept drift in the wild?

To answer these questions, I work with variational meta-learning, prototypical networks, gradient-boosted models, and modern deep-learning frameworks on malware and benchmark datasets.
